The Divine Birth

Celebrating the Eighth Avatar

Krishna Janmashtami marks the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Born in the prison of Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva at midnight on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha in the month of Bhadrapada.

The divine infant was miraculously transported across the raging Yamuna river to Gokul, where he was raised by Yashoda and Nanda. This festival symbolizes the triumph of righteousness over wickedness and divine intervention in the mortal realm.

Sacred Traditions

1

Midnight Pooja

Devotees fast until midnight, the exact moment of Krishna's birth, when special prayers and offerings are made to welcome the divine incarnation.

2

Dahi Handi

Teams form human pyramids to break clay pots filled with curd, reenacting Krishna's playful butter-thief legends with the gopis of Vrindavan.

3

Jhulanotsav

Decorated swings cradle idols of Radha-Krishna as devotees take turns gently rocking them while singing devotional songs and bhajans.

Sacred Temples

Banke Bihari Temple, Vrindavan

The most sacred shrine dedicated to Krishna in his youthful form, known for its unique darshan timings and ecstatic devotional atmosphere.

Dwarkadhish Temple, Dwarka

The legendary kingdom where Krishna established his rule after leaving Mathura, featuring ancient architecture and spiritual significance.

ISKCON Temples Worldwide

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness operates hundreds of temples globally celebrating Janmashtami with grand festivities.
